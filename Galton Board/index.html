Galton Board

More Left More Right
Learning point:

A Galton Board, also called a bean machine, is a physical device created by Francis Galton to illustrate probability principles and demonstrate the Central Limit Theorem. Each individual ball follows only one random left or right path, so a single outcome is unpredictable. However, when many balls are released together, their combined outcomes form a predictable bell-shaped distribution (shown by the red graph). Most outcomes gather near the center, while fewer outcomes appear at the extreme tails.

The more balls that are released, the clearer the bell-shaped pattern becomes. If only a few balls are used, the pattern may look uneven or random because chance has a bigger effect. As more and more balls are added, the random differences balance out, and the results become more stable and predictable. This is why larger sample sizes usually give more reliable results that follow a normal distribution in statistics and scientific research.
