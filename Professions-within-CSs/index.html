🛡️ Prevention

Prevention aims to reduce the risk of communication and swallowing disorders by strengthening underlying functions, limiting exposure to harmful environments, and educating communities.

🧪 Assessment

Assessment involves identifying strengths, limitations, and contextual barriers to communication. This includes reviewing cognitive, auditory, and behavioral functions to guide further steps.

🩺 Diagnosis

Diagnosis integrates assessment results to classify a disorder, define severity, guide intervention planning, and refer to appropriate services as needed.

💡 Treatment

Treatment focuses on building communication abilities, minimizing deficits, and promoting participation using direct intervention, strategy training, and environmental supports.

👂 Audiologists

Audiologists specialize in preventing, diagnosing, and treating hearing and balance disorders. They are integral to team-based care for those with communication challenges.

📘 ASHA

The American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA) sets standards for education, ethics, and clinical practice in communication sciences and disorders.
