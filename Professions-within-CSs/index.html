📘 Clinical Practice Topics

🛡️ Prevention

Prevention involves proactive strategies to stop communication and swallowing disorders from developing. This includes early screenings, health education, and reducing exposure to risk factors.

🧪 Assessment

Assessment is the process of gathering information about an individual’s communication abilities through tests, observations, and interviews. It helps determine the presence and severity of a disorder.

🩺 Diagnosis

Diagnosis is a professional judgment based on assessment data. It determines whether a communication disorder exists, names the condition, and guides next steps for treatment or referral.

💡 Treatment / Intervention

Treatment refers to evidence-based interventions that aim to improve or restore communication abilities. It may include therapy sessions, family education, and environmental modifications.

👂 Audiologists

Audiologists are professionals who specialize in hearing and balance disorders. They conduct hearing tests, diagnose hearing loss, and provide hearing aids and other interventions.

📘 ASHA

ASHA (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association) sets national standards for certification and ethics. It provides resources for professionals and ensures quality care for clients.
